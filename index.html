The Origins of Progressive Rock in England
Progressive rock had its beginnings in the UK, starting with bands such as Procol Harum, the Nice, and Van der Graff Generator. These proto-prog bands tended to have softer, and less intense sounds, and the songs were far longer than their contemporaries, ranging from 5 to 17 minutes. Many of the people involved in these early bands moved onto other bands in the later years as prog rock matured, such as Keith Emerson leaving the Nice and Greg Lake leaving King Crimson and forming ELP with Carl Palmer. Some of the best examples of this early period are 1969's 21st Century Schizoid Man by King Crimson, featuring Robert Fripp's excellent fuzzed guitar riffs and a horn section, Procol Harum's A Whiter Shade Of Pale, an amazing organ ballad from 1967 with a long connected composition. One of the first "long" prog rock songs is Iron Butterfly's 1968 monster of a piece In-A-Gadda-Da-Vida, a collection of guitar, drum solos, and psychedelic riffs.
