The Japanese Progressive Rock Scene Begins
Parallel to the blooming progressive rock scene in England and North America, Japan had a similar but distictively different sound, rooted in the jazz found throughout post WW2 Japan as opposed to the classical roots found in the western development of the genre. Bands such as Flied Egg, who incorporated the unique sounds of Japanese blues into a progressive context, allowed the Japanese prog scene to have a tonally distinctive sound from their counterparts. Other groups such as Outer Limits created some must listen pieces for any fan of progressive rock, from the 1987 prog classic The Scene of Pale Blue to 2007's Algo_Rythym.C.
The Zheul Revival
Around 2005, the strange genre pioneered by Christian Vander in France had a strange revival in Japan. Bands such as Koenji Hyakkei brought the frenetic other worldly sounds to another audience in Japan.
Contemporary Japanese Progressive Rock Groups
Contemporary Japanese prog rock is an incredibly diverse genre, from the jazz based concept albums of ptf, to the compositional masterworks of Electric Asturias (also known as Asturias, and Acoustic Asturias), Japanese prog rock is an ever-growing and amazing music scene.
